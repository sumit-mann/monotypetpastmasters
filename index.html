What happens in a Toastmasters meeting

A Toastmasters meeting is designed with the idea of providing platform for members to practise public speaking and leadership skills.

Every meet has three sections; a prepared speech section, a table topics sections (Extempore speeches); an evaluation section. There are around 10 different roles played by members to conduct these three sections of a Toastmasters meet effectively. Members nominate for the roles, prepare, rehearse and finally present themselves on the day of the meet.

Majorly there are two tracks that members cover through the system.

Anybody can visit our club as a visitor (guest) and observe the entire meet. One observation leads to an enormous exposure to Toastmastering.

Our Meetings

Each week, we meet to improve our communication skills. There are five main aspects to our meetings:

PREPARED SPEECHES
You will be able to enjoy speeches prepared by the club members, with the aim of improving different aspects of public speaking, which are included in the manuals: Structure, Body Language, Vocal Variety, Storytelling, Humour, Salesâ€¦
TABLE TOPICS
Imagine: Stepping-up to make a mini speech about a topic that you will not even know about previously, in front of an audience! An excellent opportunity to improve improvisation, temper your nerves and learn to think very quick.
FEEDBACK
Feedback between members helps to reinforce those things we are best at, and to focus more in those things we still need to improve. Learning to perform positive and constructive feedback is fundamental in our club.
ROLES
Today you are the presenter of the session and tomorrow you will be the timekeeper. There are several roles that need to be covered every session, and these are done by different club members. This way, we all improve our communication and leadership skills.
APPLAUSE
You better be ready: in TM we applaud, a lot! We believe that each public presentation deserves it. Applauses is our way to tell each other: well done! Keep it up!

How to join?

Start with a visit to one of our meetings

A member of the club will welcome you and will explain to you everything you need to know about Toastmasters, how the club works and how to start the process.

The sessions are free for the guests. You don't need to participate if you prefer not to talk.

Once you are ready to join us

To join us, we will ask you to fill out a form with your contact details.

There is a onetime payment when you become a member, covering the cost of 2 books that are used during the learning program: Competent Communication Manual and Competent Leadership Manual.

You have to pay a biannual fee, which includes the affiliation to Toastmasters International and you will also receive the monthly magazine

What happens when you are a member?

As a new member, you will receive:

  • The 2 first communication and leadership manuals.
  • A mentor that will guide you in the club and will help you in the preparation of your 3 first speeches.

As a member, you will have to:

  • Confirm your attendance (or absence) for every meeting.
  • Attend the meetings on a regular basis.
  • Participate with one of the roles to start improving your leadership and communication skills.

We expect to see you soon!
